Unlocking Insights from the
Stack Overflow Annual Developer Survey

Results
Through the rigorous analysis of the Stack Overflow Annual Developer Survey, several key findings emerged:

  1. Average Salary by Country: The analysis revealed significant disparities in developer salaries across different countries, with certain regions showing higher remuneration for developers.
  2. Python Adoption: Python emerged as one of the most popular programming languages among developers, highlighting its widespread adoption and versatility in various domains.
  3. Problem-Solving Resources: Developers predominantly relied on online forums, documentation, and peer networks for troubleshooting and problem-solving, underscoring the importance of community support in the developer ecosystem.
  4. Developer Salaries vs. Age/Gender: Analysis showcased nuanced trends in developer salaries concerning age and gender, offering insights into potential factors influencing compensation disparities within the industry.

Unlocking Insights from the
Big Mart Sales Data

Results
  1. Visualization of Sales Data: The notebook aims to visualize sales data from BigMart, one of Europe's largest retailers, focusing on sales trends in 2011.
  2. Data Import and Overview: The notebook begins by importing necessary libraries and reading the provided CSV file into a pandas DataFrame. It then checks the first few rows of the DataFrame and provides information about its structure and data types.
  3. Statistical Summary: Statistical summary including count, mean, standard deviation, minimum, maximum, and quartile information is provided for numerical columns like Quantity, UnitPrice, and Amount.
  4. Top Selling Items: It identifies the top ten items sold in the United Kingdom and the USA, based on quantity sold.
  5. Top Contributing Countries: The notebook determines the top ten countries contributing the most towards sales. The United Kingdom emerges as the top contributor, followed by EIRE, Netherlands, Germany, and France.
  6. Sales Trends Over Months: It visualizes the total sales per month, demonstrating how sales have fluctuated over the months in 2011. The highest sales occur in December, with a noticeable increase towards the end of the year.
  7. Comparison of Sales Visualization: It compares two visualization methods: a line plot and a bar plot, for visualizing sales per month in 2011. It suggests that while both are effective, the line plot may provide a clearer understanding of sales fluctuations.
  8. Pie Chart Analysis: A pie chart is created to illustrate the contribution of different countries towards sales in 2011. It shows that the United Kingdom contributes the highest percentage towards sales, followed by the Netherlands, EIRE, Germany, France, and the USA.
